# /consulting/case-studies/ — STUB

Selected engagement write-ups, framed for external audiences (prospects, marketing, talks).

**Status:** Stub.

## When this gets filled

After an engagement closes and you have written client permission (or a fully anonymized version). Don't write case studies *during* engagements — survivorship bias and live-deal risk make them unreliable.

## Source material

Each case study is derived from a closed engagement folder: the SOW, status reports, retro, and the outcomes record. The case study is the *outward-facing* version, with sensitive specifics removed.

## Structure (when first one is written)

- The client's situation (without naming if anonymized).
- What we did.
- The outcome (quantified where possible).
- What was hard / what we'd do differently.

The "what was hard" section is what separates a case study from marketing copy. Skip it and the document becomes worthless to the reader you're trying to win.
